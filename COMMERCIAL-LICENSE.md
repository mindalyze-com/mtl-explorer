## When do you need a commercial license?

You need a commercial license if **any** of the following apply and you do
not want to comply with the AGPL:

- You want to **offer MyTrailLog as a hosted service (SaaS)** to third parties
  without publishing your modifications and your full corresponding source
  code (including changes to the server, APIs, frontend, tiles pipeline, etc.)
  under AGPL-3.0.
- You want to **embed MyTrailLog (or any part of it) in a proprietary product**
  (including mobile apps, firmware, in-vehicle systems, outdoor hardware,
  closed-source SDKs) and distribute that product under terms other than AGPL.
- You want to **combine MyTrailLog with code under a license that is
  incompatible with AGPL-3.0** and distribute the combined work.
- You want to **remove the AGPL network-use clause obligations** (e.g. the
  requirement that users interacting with the application over a network be
  offered the corresponding source code).
- You require a **commercial warranty, indemnification, SLA, or support
  contract** that the AGPL explicitly disclaims.

Self-hosting MyTrailLog for your **own private, personal, or internal
non-commercial use** is always free under the AGPL and does **not** require a
commercial license.

## When do you NOT need a commercial license?

You do **not** need a commercial license to:

- Run MyTrailLog on your own computer or home server for personal use.
- Fork, modify, and redistribute MyTrailLog under the AGPL-3.0, **provided**
  you publish your complete corresponding source code under the same license
  and preserve all copyright and license notices.
- Contribute improvements back to the upstream project (see
  [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)).
- Run MyTrailLog inside a company for purely internal, non-public, employee-
  only use — as long as no third parties (customers, partners, the public)
  interact with it over a network. (If in doubt, contact us.)
